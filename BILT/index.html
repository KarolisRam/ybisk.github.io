Broad & Inclusive Language Technology (BILT) @ CMU LTI

The Broad & Inclusive Language Technology (BILT) Workshop will be hosted by the Carnegie Mellon Language Technologies Institute, spearheaded by professors Bisk, Strubell & Neubig.

Our two week, 12 student workshop provides a hands-on introduction to state-of-the-art language technology research, to prepare traditionally under-represented undergraduate students for success in research and graduate studies. Technologies best serve those who build them. We aim to empower students from diverse backgrounds to build tools that are specific to their needs and community, including low resourced languages, multilingual code-switching, AAVE, and assistive technologies for hearing or visually impaired.

Our workshop (1) Introduces students to modern research (e.g. speech, natural language understanding, multimodal reasoning) -- by balancing academic instruction (the why/what) and hands-on personal research projects (the how); (2) Prepares students for graduate study via direct mentorship and training; (3) Provides students with a supportive environment and network of mentors and peers.
